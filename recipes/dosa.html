Masala Dosa

Description

A classic South Indian staple, Masala Dosa consists of a thin, ultra-crispy fermented rice and lentil crepe. It is traditionally stuffed with a savory, spiced potato filling known as aloo masala.

Served piping hot alongside tangy sambar and cool coconut chutney, it offers a wonderful contrast of textures and comforting spices.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups Dosa batter (fermented rice and urad dal blend)
  • 3 medium Potatoes (boiled and roughly mashed)
  • 1 medium Onion (sliced)
  • 2 Green chilies (finely chopped)
  • 1/2 tsp Mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp Turmeric powder
  • A sprig of fresh Curry leaves
  • Oil or Ghee for roasting

Steps

  1. Prepare Filling: Heat oil, temper with mustard seeds and curry leaves. Sauté onions and chilies until soft.
  2. Add turmeric, salt, mashed potatoes, and a splash of water. Mix well and cook for 3 minutes to form a cohesive filling. Set aside.
  3. Heat a non-stick tawa or flat griddle. Splash water to cool it slightly and wipe clean.
  4. Pour a ladleful of batter in the center and spread it quickly outward in a spiral motion to form a thin crepe.
  5. Drizzle ghee or oil around the edges and cook on medium heat until the bottom turns completely golden brown and crispy.
  6. Place a portion of the potato filling in the center, fold the dosa over, and serve immediately. Enjoy!
